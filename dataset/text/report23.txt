Patient Care Report 23: Brian Smith, 55-year-old male experiencing an allergic reaction after a bee sting to his right hand. He presents with hives, swelling of the lips, and reports difficulty swallowing. Past medical history includes seasonal allergies and a previous mild reaction to insect stings. Medications: loratadine. Allergic to penicillin. Vitals en route: BP 100/60, HR 115, RR 24 with mild stridor, SpO2 93% on room air. We administered 0.3 mg epinephrine intramuscularly, started oxygen at 10 liters per minute via non-rebreather mask, and established IV access with normal saline.
